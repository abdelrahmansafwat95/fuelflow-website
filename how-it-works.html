End-to-end

From the pump to the dashboard.

Here's what actually happens when a driver pulls in. Every step matters — and every one is under 200ms.

1

Vehicle pulls in

Driver arrives at a FuelFlow-enabled station. Could be any forecourt — single site or a chain.

2

Driver opens app, scans pump QR

The QR encodes station_id + pump_id. Driver identity is bound to the device + vehicle assignment.

3

App calls POST /authorize/scan

Encrypted over TLS. Payload includes vehicle, driver, pump, geolocation, expected fuel type.

4

Transaction service runs checks

All four happen in parallel. Any failure short-circuits the request.

  • Vehicle is active and assigned to this driver
  • Budget is available (Redis atomic reservation, no race conditions)
  • Station is authorized for this company
  • Fraud checks pass (6 rules in parallel)
5

APPROVED → pump unlocks

FCC signal sent to the pump controller. Driver sees the green light. Total elapsed time: < 200ms.

6

Driver fills up

Attendant confirms litres on the Attendant App, or — where integrated — the meter reports directly.

7

Meter reports actual litres → POST /authorize/complete

Final amount, fuel type, timestamp. The reservation closes.

8

Budget moves: reserved → spent

Atomic Postgres transaction. Audit log entry committed with actor, before/after, timestamp.

9

Fleet manager sees the receipt instantly

Server-Sent Events push the transaction into the dashboard before the driver walks away from the pump.

10

Alerts fire if needed

Budget > 80%? Anomaly detected? Station offline? Alert lands in Slack, email, or in-app — wherever you've configured.
