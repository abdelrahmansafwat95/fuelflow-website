About FuelFlow

Built in Egypt, for Egypt.

Every corporate litre, accountable. That's the bar — and it's why FuelFlow exists.

Why we built this

Egyptian corporate fuel runs on cash slips and Excel. Fleet managers spend the first week of each month reconciling receipts from the previous month, and they still can't tell a real fill-up from a sold-on-the-side litre. We've watched founders, accountants, and operations leads burn time on a problem that's solvable.

FuelFlow is the platform we wished existed when we were on the other side of the table — running fleets, managing forecourts, signing the cheques.

Our mission

Make every corporate litre accountable. Real-time, end-to-end, with the rough edges smoothed for the people who actually do the work — drivers at the pump, attendants on the forecourt, finance teams at month-end.

What we believe

  • Egypt-first means Arabic-first. Not a translation layer. Originals, RTL, EGP, +20 phone numbers.
  • Show, don't claim. We'd rather demo than pitch. The numbers are in the dashboard.
  • Security is a product feature. Procurement reviews aren't an obstacle — they're a chance to prove the engineering.
  • Operators know best. We listen to fleet managers, attendants, and station owners. The roadmap comes from the forecourt, not the office.

Where we are

Cairo, Egypt. The team is local — engineering, sales, and customer success all in-country. We hire for ownership and for empathy with the Egyptian operator.

Under the hood

Built on technology we can defend.

We don't lead with the stack — but if you're curious, here's what runs FuelFlow.

Backend

Node.js (Express) microservices in TypeScript. PostgreSQL 16, Redis 7, TimescaleDB for analytics.

Apps

React (Vite) for the web. React Native (Expo Router) for the Driver and Attendant apps.

Operations

Docker, Nginx with TLS, Prometheus + Grafana for observability. Real-time via Redis pub/sub + SSE.
